Welcome to All In

All In is an interactive awareness experience designed to help people understand the complex dynamics of gambling addiction and its impact on relationships.

Start the Interactive Story

How It Works

Through realistic scenarios, you'll face decisions that highlight how gambling addiction affects not just the individual, but their friends, family, and community.

Learning Objectives

  • Recognize signs of gambling addiction
  • Understand how to support someone with a gambling problem
  • Learn about healthy boundaries and decision-making
  • Discover available resources and support systems

  • Emergency Services: 911

Getting Help

For Yourself

If you think you might have a gambling problem:

  • Take an honest self-assessment
  • Reach out to a trusted friend or family member
  • Contact a professional counselor or helpline
  • Consider joining a support group

For Someone You Care About

If you're concerned about someone else's gambling:

  • Express your concerns without judgment
  • Offer specific support and resources
  • Set healthy boundaries for yourself
  • Seek support for yourself as well

Quick Self-Assessment

Answer these questions honestly:

  1. Do you think about gambling often?
  2. Have you tried to cut back or stop gambling but couldn't?
  3. Do you gamble to escape problems or feelings?
  4. Have you lied about your gambling activities?
  5. Has gambling caused problems in your relationships or work?

If you answered yes to any of these questions, consider reaching out for support.
